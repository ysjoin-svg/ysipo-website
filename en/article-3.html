Complete Guide to PCT International Patent Filing: Building a Global IP Portfolio from Taiwan

Principal Li Tsung-Ying February 28, 2026 ~10 min read

Taiwan inventors and companies increasingly use the PCT system to secure international patent protection efficiently. A single PCT application can preserve your rights in up to 157 member countries ??if you understand how to use it strategically.

For Taiwan companies planning to enter international markets, the question "Should we use PCT or file directly in each country?" is one of the most frequently asked. The answer depends on your target markets, budget timeline, and business schedule ??but in most situations, PCT provides valuable breathing room: time to assess an invention's commercial potential before committing to the substantial costs of individual national filings.

What Is PCT?

The Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) is an international treaty administered by the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), currently with 157 member countries. Through a single PCT application, an applicant can simultaneously preserve the priority date in all PCT member countries, then decide ??within a set deadline (typically 30 months) ??which countries to enter in the national phase.

PCT does not directly grant a patent. It is a procedural framework that allows you to complete international preliminary examination first, then pursue individual national filings as your commercial needs dictate. The core advantage is the time it buys for decision-making.

PCT's core advantage: One application covers 157 member countries, and delays national phase entry by up to 30 months ??giving you time to assess market potential before making the investment decision.

PCT Filing Timeline: Key Milestones

Here is the complete process timeline for a PCT application filed from Taiwan:

M0

File Priority Application (Taiwan or other country)

File an invention patent application with TIPO to establish your priority date.

M12

File PCT Application (within 12 months of priority date)

The PCT application must be filed within 12 months of the priority date to claim that priority. This is the most critical deadline ??missing it cannot be remedied.

M18

International Publication (18 months from priority date)

The PCT application is automatically published at 18 months. After publication, competitors can see your technical disclosure ??but your protection scope is also established.

M30

National/Regional Phase Entry (30 months from priority date)

You must decide which countries to enter before the 30-month deadline (some offices such as EPO and USPTO have their own rules ??confirm in advance). National phase entry is when the bulk of translation costs and national filing fees are incurred.

PCT vs. Direct National Filing: How to Choose

PCT is not a universal solution. In some situations, filing directly in each target country is more efficient:

When PCT Makes Sense

  • Multiple target markets (typically 3 or more), and you have not finalized which countries to enter
  • You want to defer large national filing costs by 12??8 months to wait for market validation
  • You need the International Search Report (ISR) as an internal patentability assessment
  • The technology is complex and requires time to prepare country-specific prosecution strategies

When Direct National Filing Makes Sense

  • Target markets are clear and limited (1?? countries)
  • You need to obtain a grant as quickly as possible in a specific market
  • A competitor may already be filing similar inventions ??you need to move fast
"PCT gives applicants valuable time to make investment decisions when the commercial picture is clearer. But time itself does not strengthen a patent. What matters is using that time to prepare properly for national phase prosecution."

Common PCT Target Markets for Taiwan Companies

Based on YSIPO's experience, the markets Taiwan companies most frequently target through PCT national phase entry include:

  • United States (USPTO): The most important market ??virtually every company with international IP ambitions enters the US.
  • China (CNIPA): Both a manufacturing base and a major consumer market ??especially critical for Taiwan tech companies.
  • Japan (JPO): A key node in the technology partnership and supply chain ecosystem.
  • Europe (EPO): A single EPO filing can extend protection across multiple EU member states.
  • Southeast Asia: Depending on product markets ??Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia are common choices.

The right strategy depends on your product markets, competitive landscape, budget, and overall IP portfolio objectives. There is no universal answer ??but one principle is constant: the earlier you plan, the more options you have.

PCT Cost Structure

PCT costs occur in phases (figures are approximate and vary with exchange rates and office fee schedules):

  • PCT Filing Stage: WIPO international filing fee, transmittal fee, and search fee (approximately USD 2,000??,000)
  • Pre-Publication: Additional International Preliminary Examination fee if requested
  • National Phase Entry: Each country has its own official fees and agent fees ??US approximately USD 2,000??,000; Europe approximately EUR 2,000??,000; China and Japan each have their own structures

Cost planning guidance: For simultaneous entry into the US, China, Europe, and Japan, the total international patent portfolio cost ??from Taiwan filing through national phase grants ??typically ranges from NTD 800,000 to NTD 2,000,000 or more, depending on technology complexity and the number of prosecution rounds. Conclusion: International IP Strategy Requires Early Planning

PCT is a powerful tool, but its effective use requires precise timing and strategic thinking. From technology completion to Taiwan filing, to PCT filing, to national phase entry ??every milestone has a statutory deadline. Every strategic decision must account for market conditions, budget, and competitive dynamics.
