New Type Patent vs Invention Patent: Choosing the Most Suitable Protection Method

Introduction to Patent Types

In Taiwan, patent protection is a crucial aspect of intellectual property law, allowing inventors and businesses to safeguard their innovative ideas and creations. The Taiwan Intellectual Property Office offers two primary types of patents: new type patents and invention patents. Understanding the differences between these two types of patents is essential for selecting the most suitable protection method. New type patents are designed for minor innovations or improvements to existing products, whereas invention patents are geared towards more substantial and groundbreaking inventions. The choice between these two types of patents depends on the nature of the invention, the level of innovation, and the desired scope of protection.

Key Considerations for Patent Selection

When deciding between a new type patent and an invention patent, several factors come into play. Firstly, the level of innovation is a critical consideration. If the invention is a significant improvement or a novel solution to a problem, an invention patent may be more suitable. On the other hand, if the invention is an incremental improvement or a minor modification to an existing product, a new type patent might be more appropriate. Additionally, the scope of protection is another important factor. Invention patents generally offer broader protection, covering not only the specific invention but also similar inventions that perform the same function. In contrast, new type patents have a narrower scope of protection, focusing on the specific embodiment of the invention. The duration of protection is also a key consideration, with invention patents typically having a longer protection period of 20 years, compared to 10 years for new type patents.

Practical Advice for Business Owners and Inventors

For business owners and inventors in Taiwan, it is essential to carefully evaluate their inventions and choose the most suitable patent type. To increase the chances of successful patent application, it is recommended to conduct a thorough search of existing patents and literature to ensure the invention is novel and non-obvious. Furthermore, working with a patent attorney or IP expert can help navigate the complex patent application process and ensure that the chosen patent type aligns with the invention's characteristics. It is also crucial to consider the costs associated with each type of patent, as well as the potential benefits and return on investment. By selecting the most suitable patent type, business owners and inventors can effectively protect their intellectual property, preventing unauthorized use and exploitation, and ultimately driving innovation and growth.
